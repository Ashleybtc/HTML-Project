My favourite meal is Beans Porridge

About My Favourite Meal

Nigerian beans porridge, also known as stewed beans, is a dietary staple in many households in the country. It is called “ewa riro” in Yoruba or sometimes just referred to as “ewa”, which means beans. It is similar to the Ghanaian “red red” and is perfect for vegetarians. Most Nigerian beans recipes usually involve one of three beans which all belong to the Vigna unguiculata family and play a crucial role in the Nigerian diet. They are, ewa oloyin, drum and Sokoto white. It takes about one hour five minutes to cook

A List of Steps to Prepare kggs

  1. Rinse the beans and place in a deep pot with 7 cups of water.
  2. Boil the beans on medium heat until it becomes soft, it could take about 40 mins. If you are using black eyed peas, it might take a little longer.
  3. While the beans are boiling, blend the peppers, and onions and set aside
  4. Once the beans are softened from boiling (do not drain the water), turn the heat down to low, the pour in the blended tomatoes into pot.
  5. Allow the beans to cook for an additional 10 minutes with the pepper mix, then add in the bullion and palm oil. Stir, and cook for another 10 minutes.
  6. Season the beans with salt to your taste. Turn off the heat, and allow to sit for 5 minutes before serving
